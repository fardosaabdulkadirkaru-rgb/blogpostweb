Introduction: Who is Nuria Gollo H.S.C.?

October 15, 2023

Welcome to my blog, where I share my journey as a beacon of resilience and leadership in the Borana indigenous community of Northern Kenya. My name is Nuria Gollo H.S.C., and I am proud to stand as a champion for women's rights and community development. Born and raised in the heart of the Borana lands, I have witnessed firsthand the challenges faced by women and marginalized communities in our region. Yet, through perseverance and unwavering dedication, I have transformed these experiences into a force for positive change.

My Background

As a dedicated educator, I began my career nurturing young minds in local schools, instilling values of empowerment and self-belief. However, my path led me beyond the classroom into grassroots activism. I founded and lead MWADO (Marsabit Women Advocacy and Development Organization), an organization committed to uplifting women and fostering sustainable development. My work focuses on addressing critical issues such as gender-based violence, female genital mutilation (FGM), and access to education and healthcare for indigenous communities.

In Kenya, where cultural traditions often intersect with modern challenges, I advocate for a balance that honors our heritage while promoting equality. "Tunaweza!" – We can do it! – is a phrase that resonates deeply with me. It encapsulates the spirit of possibility that drives my efforts. Despite facing adversity, including societal barriers and personal losses, I have remained steadfast in my mission to create a better future for the Borana people and women across Kenya.

Key Milestones in My Life

  • Early Education: Overcoming limited resources to pursue higher learning and become an educator.
  • Founding MWADO: Establishing an organization to amplify women's voices and drive community initiatives.
  • National Recognition: Receiving accolades like the Hero’s Award and Head of State Commendation for my advocacy work.
  • Global Advocacy: Sharing our stories on international platforms to raise awareness about indigenous women's rights.

Why I'm Running

As I step into this new chapter of leadership, I am motivated by a vision of a Kenya where every woman and child has the opportunity to thrive. My experiences have taught me that true change comes from the ground up, through community engagement and collective action. In this blog, I will delve deeper into my vision, my journey, and the impact of our work. Join me as we explore how we can build a more just and empowered society.
