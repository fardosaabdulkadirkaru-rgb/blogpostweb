Impact and Achievements: Recognition on a Global Stage

October 30, 2023

The impact of our work at MWADO extends far beyond the Borana community, reaching national and international audiences. Through tireless advocacy, we've not only transformed lives but also garnered recognition that amplifies our message. From local communities to global platforms, our achievements demonstrate the power of grassroots activism in driving meaningful change.

National Recognition

In Kenya, our efforts have been honored with prestigious awards that validate our commitment to women's rights and community development. The Hero’s Award and Head of State Commendation stand as testaments to the tangible differences we've made in addressing issues like female genital mutilation (FGM) and gender-based violence. These accolades have opened doors for collaboration with government agencies and other NGOs, allowing us to scale our programs and influence policy.

Our work has led to increased awareness and action against harmful practices. Communities that once resisted change now participate in our initiatives, resulting in reduced FGM rates and improved access to education and healthcare for women and girls.

International Influence

On the global stage, our story has resonated with audiences worldwide. Features in The Guardian and CBC Canada Radio have brought attention to the challenges faced by indigenous women in Kenya and the innovative solutions we're implementing. These platforms have helped us connect with international supporters, funders, and advocates who share our vision for a more equitable world.

Through partnerships with global organizations, we've contributed to broader discussions on women's rights, indigenous rights, and sustainable development. Our influence extends to policy recommendations and advocacy at international forums, where Kenyan voices are increasingly heard.

Key Achievements

  • Hero’s Award (2015): Recognition for outstanding community leadership and anti-FGM efforts.
  • Head of State Commendation (2020): Presidential acknowledgment of our work in women's empowerment.
  • The Guardian Feature (2018): International coverage highlighting our grassroots approach.
  • CBC Canada Radio Interview (2021): Global broadcast sharing our story and impact.
  • Community Impact: Reduced FGM in targeted areas by 40%, increased women's participation in local governance.
  • Program Expansion: Grown MWADO from a small group to a network serving thousands across Northern Kenya.

Measuring Our Impact

To quantify our achievements, we've tracked key metrics that reflect the real-world changes we're creating:

Area Achievement Impact
Anti-FGM Campaigns 40% reduction in targeted communities Improved health outcomes for women and girls
Education Programs 500+ girls enrolled in secondary schools Increased literacy and future opportunities
Healthcare Access 3 new clinics established Better maternal and reproductive health services
Economic Empowerment 200 women trained in business skills Financial independence and community growth
Policy Advocacy Influenced 5 local policies Stronger legal protections for women's rights

The Road Ahead

While we're proud of our achievements, we know there's more work to do. Each recognition fuels our determination to continue advocating for change. "Pamoja Tutashinda!" – Together we will win! – as we build on these successes to create an even greater impact. In the final post of this series, I'll share how you can join our movement and support our ongoing efforts.
