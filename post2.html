Vision & Goals: Empowering Women and Communities

October 20, 2023

My vision is simple yet profound: a world where every woman in Kenya, especially in indigenous communities like the Borana, lives with dignity, equality, and opportunity. As a leader in women's rights and community development, I am committed to creating sustainable change that uplifts individuals and strengthens our society. Through MWADO, we work tirelessly to address the root causes of inequality and empower women to take control of their futures.

Commitment to Women's Empowerment

Empowerment begins with recognizing the inherent strength and potential within each woman. In Northern Kenya, where traditional roles often limit opportunities, I advocate for education, economic independence, and political participation. My goal is to break down barriers that prevent women from contributing fully to their communities. By fostering leadership skills and providing resources, we can transform lives and build a more inclusive society.

Key to this vision is addressing gender-based violence and harmful practices like female genital mutilation (FGM). These issues not only harm individuals but also perpetuate cycles of poverty and inequality. "Pamoja Tutashinda!" – Together we will win! – is our rallying cry as we work towards a Kenya free from such injustices.

Key Issues We Aim to Address

Our work focuses on several critical areas that impact women's lives and community development:

  • Education: Ensuring access to quality education for girls and women, reducing dropout rates and promoting lifelong learning.
  • Healthcare: Improving maternal health services, combating diseases, and providing support for victims of gender-based violence.
  • Gender-Based Violence: Creating safe spaces, legal protections, and awareness campaigns to end violence against women.
  • Economic Empowerment: Offering training, microloans, and opportunities for women to start businesses and achieve financial independence.
  • Cultural Preservation: Honoring Borana traditions while promoting practices that support equality and human rights.

Our Goals for the Future

To achieve our vision, we have set ambitious yet achievable goals:

  1. Eliminate FGM in Borana communities within the next decade through education and community engagement.
  2. Increase female enrollment in secondary education by 50% in targeted areas.
  3. Establish 10 new women's health clinics across Northern Kenya.
  4. Train 1,000 women in leadership and entrepreneurship skills annually.
  5. Advocate for policy changes that protect indigenous women's rights at the national level.
